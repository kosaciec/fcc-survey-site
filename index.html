Maria Kelles-Krauz

The First Chairwoman of a City Council in Poland
    Here's a time line of Maria Kelles-Krauz's life:
  • 1882 - born in Radom as Maria Nynkowska
  • 1904 - marries Stanis≈Çaw Kelles-Krauz, a doctor and a member of the Polish Socialist Party
  • 1915 - 1919 is a president of the Polish Women's League of War Emergency in Radom, an organization that supported women's rights and Polish independence
  • 1919 - after regaining independence and first local elections in Poland she becomes a member of the city council of Radom. She is elected the first chairwoman of a city council in Poland (and probably in the world). She holds her office for two terms
  • 1922 - initiates founding of a public library in Radom
  • 1927 - receives the Knight's Cross of Order of Polonia Restituta for her social activism
  • WW II - under Nazi occupation, she organizes for Polish children an underground education of foridden subjects in her own house
  • 1946 - goes with her husband to Denmark where she learns Danish language and translates Danish literature to Polish
  • 1954 - returns to Poland, settles down in Warsaw and works at Polish Red Cross
  • 1969 - dies in Warsaw at the age of 87.

"If Radom was surprised of my election, then I was simply terrified. Can I manage, will I not discredit women?"

-- Maria Kelles-Krauz about the election for chairwoman.
